Medicare Eligibility

Who is eligible for Medicare and our Senior Care Plus plan?

Medicare eligibility generally includes people who are:

  • Age 65 or older
  • Under 65 with qualifying disabilities
  • Any age with End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) or ALS

To be eligible for our Senior Care Plus Medicare Supplement plan, you need to:

  • Be enrolled in Medicare Parts A & B
  • Live in our service area
  • Not have Medicaid or other supplemental coverage

The Medicare Annual Election Period typically runs from October 15 to December 7 each year. During this time, you can enroll in or change your Medicare Advantage or Part D plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does this plan work with Medicare?

The Senior Care Plus plan works alongside your Original Medicare (Parts A & B). Medicare will pay its share of the Medicare-approved amounts for covered healthcare costs, and then our plan pays its share. This provides more comprehensive coverage and reduces your out-of-pocket expenses.

Can I keep my current doctor?

Yes, you can see any doctor or healthcare provider that accepts Medicare. Our plan works with Medicare, so any provider that accepts Medicare can provide services to you. However, you may save more by using in-network providers who participate in our Premium Provider Network.

What's the difference between this and Medicare Advantage?

Medicare Supplement plans (like our Senior Care Plus) work alongside Original Medicare, while Medicare Advantage plans replace Original Medicare. With our supplement plan, you can see any provider that accepts Medicare, whereas Medicare Advantage plans typically have more restricted networks. Our plan also has no referral requirements to see specialists, unlike many Medicare Advantage plans.

Does this plan cover prescription drugs?

Yes, our Senior Care Plus plan includes enhanced prescription drug coverage. We offer low or no copays for generic medications and reasonable costs for brand-name drugs. We also provide additional coverage during the Medicare Part D coverage gap ("donut hole") to help keep your medication costs affordable throughout the year.

When can I enroll in this plan?

You can enroll during your Initial Enrollment Period when you first become eligible for Medicare, during the Annual Election Period (October 15-December 7), or if you qualify for a Special Enrollment Period due to certain life events. The best time to enroll in a Medicare Supplement plan is during your 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment Period, which begins the month you're 65 or older and enrolled in Medicare Part B.
