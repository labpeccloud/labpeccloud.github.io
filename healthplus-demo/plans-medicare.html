Medicare Eligibility

You're eligible for our Medicare Advantage Plan if you:

Medicare Advantage FAQs

What is the difference between Original Medicare and Medicare Advantage?

Original Medicare is the traditional fee-for-service program offered directly through the federal government, consisting of Part A (hospital insurance) and Part B (medical insurance). Medicare Advantage, also known as Medicare Part C, is an alternative to Original Medicare offered by private insurance companies like HealthPlus.

Medicare Advantage plans provide all the benefits of Original Medicare plus additional coverage such as prescription drugs, dental, vision, hearing, and wellness programs, often with lower out-of-pocket costs. Unlike Original Medicare, Medicare Advantage plans have an annual limit on your out-of-pocket expenses.

Can I keep my current doctors with your Medicare Advantage plan?

You can continue seeing your current doctors if they participate in our network. HealthPlus Medicare Advantage has one of the largest provider networks, so there's a good chance your doctors are already in-network. Before enrolling, you can use our Provider Directory tool on our website to verify if your doctors participate in our network.

If your doctor is not in our network, you may still be able to see them, but your out-of-pocket costs may be higher. Some of our plans also offer out-of-network coverage, giving you more flexibility in choosing your healthcare providers.

When can I enroll in a Medicare Advantage plan?

You can enroll in a Medicare Advantage plan during these periods:

  • Initial Enrollment Period: When you first become eligible for Medicare (typically three months before the month you turn 65, the month of your birthday, and three months after)
  • Annual Enrollment Period: October 15 to December 7 each year
  • Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment Period: January 1 to March 31 each year (if you're already enrolled in a Medicare Advantage plan)
  • Special Enrollment Period: If you qualify due to certain life events like moving, losing other coverage, or qualifying for Extra Help

Our Medicare specialists can help determine the best enrollment period for your situation.

Do I still pay my Medicare Part B premium with Medicare Advantage?

Yes, you must continue paying your Medicare Part B premium in addition to any premium charged by your Medicare Advantage plan. The standard Part B premium is set by Medicare each year, and most people have this amount deducted from their Social Security benefits.

Some of our Medicare Advantage plans have $0 premiums, which means you pay no additional premium beyond your Part B premium. Other plans may have a monthly premium that provides enhanced benefits and lower out-of-pocket costs.

What happens if I need care while traveling?

All HealthPlus Medicare Advantage plans cover emergency and urgent care anywhere in the United States. If you're traveling within the U.S. and have a medical emergency or need urgent care, you can go to any hospital or urgent care facility, even if it's outside of our network.

For international travel, our plans provide emergency coverage in certain situations, typically with a higher copay or coinsurance. Some of our premium plans offer more extensive travel coverage. Before traveling internationally, we recommend contacting our Member Services team to understand your coverage details.

Can I switch back to Original Medicare if I don't like my Medicare Advantage plan?

Yes, you can switch back to Original Medicare during the Medicare Advantage Open Enrollment Period (January 1 to March 31) or during the Annual Enrollment Period (October 15 to December 7). If you switch back to Original Medicare, you might also want to enroll in a standalone Medicare Part D plan for prescription drug coverage and consider a Medicare Supplement (Medigap) policy to help cover some of the out-of-pocket costs of Original Medicare.

Keep in mind that if you switch back to Original Medicare and want to purchase a Medigap policy, you might be subject to medical underwriting, which could affect your eligibility or premium.
