Family Plan FAQs

How many family members can be covered under the Family Plan?

Our Family Plan covers you, your spouse or domestic partner, and all dependent children up to age 26, regardless of how many children you have. There is no limit to the number of family members who can be covered under one Family Plan.

How does the family deductible work?

Our Family Plan uses an aggregate deductible system, which means there are two ways to meet your deductible: either one family member meets their individual deductible (which is half of the family deductible), or multiple family members' expenses combine to reach the family deductible. Once either threshold is met, all covered services subject to the deductible will be covered for all family members for the remainder of the plan year.

What happens when my child turns 26?

When your child turns 26, they will no longer be eligible for coverage under your Family Plan. However, we'll notify you before this occurs and provide information about individual plan options for your child. They will qualify for a Special Enrollment Period, allowing them to enroll in their own plan without waiting for the annual Open Enrollment Period.

Are prenatal and maternity services covered?

Yes, all of our Family Plans include comprehensive prenatal, maternity, and postnatal care. This includes prenatal doctor visits, lab tests, ultrasounds, labor and delivery (including cesarean sections), hospital stays, and postnatal care. Newborns are automatically covered under your Family Plan for the first 30 days after birth.

Can I add a family member mid-year?

Yes, certain life events qualify you for a Special Enrollment Period, allowing you to add family members mid-year. These events include marriage, birth, adoption, or placement for adoption or foster care. You typically have 60 days from the qualifying event to add the new family member to your plan.

Is there a separate deductible for each family member?

While each family member has an individual deductible (typically set at 50% of the family deductible), our plan also has an aggregate family deductible. This means that once your family's combined medical expenses reach the family deductible amount, all family members are considered to have met their deductibles for the year, even if some individuals haven't met their personal deductibles.
